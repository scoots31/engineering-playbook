## Section 1: Project Identity

**Project Name:** Gift Tracker
**Run Type:** Full framework run
**Phases in Scope:** All phases — Discovery through Phase Test

---

## Section 2: Problem and Purpose

**Problem Statement:** Generous people — especially around the holidays — have no organized way to track who they're buying for, what they plan to spend, what they've already bought, and what it cost. This solves the planning and tracking problem so gift buying becomes intentional, not a last-minute scramble.

**Who Uses It:** Anyone with a habit of buying gifts for friends and family, particularly around holidays, who wants to stay organized and fair across the people they're buying for.

**Why It Matters:** Prevents last-minute crunch and the stress it creates. Prevents overspending. Helps users feel in control of something that usually feels chaotic.

---

## Section 3: Definition of Good

**What Good Looks Like:**
1. Gift buying is organized — a clear list of individuals with intended spend amounts visible at a glance
2. The user can see fairness across recipients — no accidental appearance of favoritism in spending
3. The user can track and revisit gift ideas for each person without losing them between sessions

**What Done Means:**
1. Clear UX to review each individual with their gift details, costs, gift idea links, budget, and notes
2. Clear UX to add a new individual to the buying list
3. Clear UX to set an overall budget and a per-individual budget
4. A view that shows at a glance which individuals still have gifts remaining to purchase

**Tone / Experience:** Light and fun — clear usability. Nothing heavy or corporate.

---

## Section 4: Drift Watchlist

**Push Back On (1):** Any feature or screen that doesn't directly serve the gift buying and tracking experience — if it's not about tracking people, gifts, costs, ideas, or budgets, it doesn't belong.

**Push Back On (2):** Any UX or UI that is cluttered, hard to navigate, or visually unappealing — this product should feel good to use, not like a spreadsheet.

**Push Back On (3):** Any attempt to defer a clearly needed feature to a later phase or debate whether it belongs in scope — if it's in this brief, it gets built.

**Out of Scope:** No integrations with external retailers (Amazon, Walmart, or similar). This is a tracker, not a shopping facilitator.
