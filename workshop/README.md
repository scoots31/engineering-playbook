# Workshop Framework

*The companion to the Solo Builder Framework — for work that isn't a product.*

Most of what a solo builder does isn't shipping a product. It's one-off scripts,
data explorations, spikes, personal CLI tools, throwaway prototypes, agents,
research notebooks. Workshop is for that shape of work.

Where the Solo Builder Framework says *process-first, gates, anchors, artifacts,*
Workshop says *outcome-first, timebox, disposable*. It is not a lite version of
SBF. It is a philosophical anti-pattern, held in deliberate tension.

---

## When to use Workshop vs SBF

One question answers it: **Who is this for, and how long does it live?**

| Shape | Who | Lifespan | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Spike** | Me, right now | Hours to days | Workshop |
| **Tool** | Me, ongoing | Personal utility | Workshop |
| **Product** | Other people | Indefinite | Solo Builder Framework |

The `scope-check` skill holds this line. If you call something a spike but describe
users, it challenges.

---

## Load-bearing principles

1. **Timebox over gates** — bound by time, not completion criteria.
2. **Outcome over process** — one question to answer or one thing to make work.
3. **Disposable by default** — assume it gets tossed unless explicitly promoted.
4. **Single artifact** — one file, one notebook, one doc. No parallel artifact sprawl.
5. **Explicit graduation** — the moment a spike becomes real, `land` forces promotion
   to SBF. No silent drift into production.

---

## The three skills

| Skill | Role |
|---|---|
| `scope-check` | Entry. Spike vs Tool vs Product. Routes to Workshop or hands to SBF `start`. |
| `spike` | The work. Timeboxed, one outcome, running journal of what was tried and what happened. |
| `land` | The exit. **Toss** (capture learning, archive), **Keep** (personal tool, minimal hygiene), or **Promote** (hand to SBF with a prepared brief). |

Three skills. The moment a fourth appears, Workshop is drifting toward SBF-lite —
which is the failure mode.

---

## What Workshop does not have

No process-mapper, no product-continuity, no framework-health, no four anchors,
no phase test, no design sprint, no always-on skills. Those assume sessions across
weeks and multi-artifact state. Spikes don't have that scale.

---

## Shared with SBF

- MemPalace capture — spikes produce knowledge, knowledge compounds across sessions
- Honest partnership principles — no sycophancy, push back, explain the why
- Optional light touch of `tech-context` if the tool's stack matters

---

## Graduation — the bridge to SBF

`land` is the load-bearing skill. Any signal that the work is crossing into product
territory — "can I share this?", "should I add auth?", "someone else wants to try
it" — triggers mandatory promotion. Workshop does not let spikes silently become
shipped products.
